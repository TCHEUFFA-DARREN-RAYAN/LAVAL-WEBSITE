FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about Laval Bilingual High School. If you can't find what you're looking for, please don't hesitate to contact us.

Laval Bilingual High School offers a truly bilingual education where students learn in both English and French. Our unique approach integrates both languages across the curriculum, rather than treating them as separate subjects. We combine the best educational practices from different systems to provide a comprehensive international education that prepares students for global opportunities.

Founded in 1985, Laval Bilingual High School was established to provide quality bilingual education to meet the growing demand for internationally-minded education. Starting with just 50 students, we have grown to a community of over 800 students from diverse backgrounds. Throughout our history, we have maintained our commitment to academic excellence, cultural understanding, and preparing students for success in a globalized world.

School hours are from 8:00 AM to 3:30 PM, Monday through Friday. The school gates open at 7:30 AM, and students are expected to arrive by 7:50 AM for the morning assembly. After-school activities typically run from 3:45 PM to 5:00 PM.

Yes, we offer a school bus service for students living within a 15-mile radius of the school. The bus routes are designed to provide convenient pickup and drop-off points. For families outside this radius, we can help coordinate carpooling options. Please contact our transportation office for more details and to register for the bus service.

We integrate technology throughout our curriculum to enhance learning and prepare students for the digital world. All classrooms are equipped with interactive whiteboards, and we maintain computer labs with up-to-date hardware and software. Students in Forms 4-6 participate in our 1:1 laptop program, while younger students have access to shared devices. We emphasize digital literacy and responsible use of technology.

Our admissions process involves several steps: 1) Submission of the application form and required documents, 2) Entrance examination to assess language proficiency and academic readiness, 3) Interview with the student and parents, and 4) Final decision and enrollment. We recommend starting the application process at least 6 months before your desired entry date. Please visit our Admissions page for detailed information and application deadlines.

While prior knowledge of both English and French is beneficial, it is not a strict requirement for all grade levels. For entry into Form 1, we look for aptitude and willingness to learn both languages. For higher grades, a certain level of proficiency in both languages is expected. We offer language support programs to help students develop their skills in either language.

Tuition fees vary by grade level: Junior Forms (1-3): $12,000 per year, Senior Forms (4-5): $14,000 per year, and Sixth Form: $16,000 per year. Additional fees include a one-time application fee of $100, an annual registration fee of $500, and fees for books, materials, technology, and activities. Please refer to our Admissions page for a complete fee schedule and payment options.

Yes, we offer both merit-based scholarships and need-based financial aid. Merit scholarships are awarded based on academic excellence, leadership potential, and special talents. Need-based financial aid is available to families who demonstrate financial need. Scholarship applications must be submitted along with the admissions application. Please contact our Admissions Office for more information about eligibility criteria and application procedures.

Yes, we accept mid-year transfers subject to availability of places and the student's ability to integrate into our curriculum. Each case is evaluated individually to ensure the student's success. We provide additional support for mid-year entrants to help them adjust to our bilingual program and catch up on any curriculum differences.

We offer a blended curriculum that incorporates elements from both international and national educational systems. In the junior forms, we follow an integrated curriculum that builds strong foundations in both English and French. In the senior forms, students prepare for internationally recognized qualifications including the International Baccalaureate (IB) Diploma Programme and/or A-Levels, with options to also take the French Baccalauréat. This approach ensures our graduates have credentials recognized by universities worldwide.

Our bilingual approach means that subjects are taught in both English and French, rather than simply offering language classes. In the junior forms, subjects alternate between the two languages throughout the week. In the senior forms, students can choose which language track they prefer for certain subjects, while maintaining proficiency in both languages. This immersive approach helps students develop true bilingualism and bicultural understanding.

Our average class size is 20 students, with a maximum of 24 students per class. This allows for personalized attention while maintaining a dynamic learning environment. For specialized subjects and advanced courses in the senior forms, class sizes may be smaller.

We have a comprehensive support system for students who need additional help. This includes after-school tutoring, peer mentoring programs, and individualized learning plans developed in collaboration with teachers, parents, and our learning support specialists. For students who need language support, we offer English as an Additional Language (EAL) and French as an Additional Language (FAL) programs. Our goal is to help every student reach their full potential.

Our students consistently achieve excellent results in external examinations. In the IB Diploma Programme, our average score is 36 points (global average: 30), with several students achieving 40+ points each year. For A-Levels, over 85% of grades are A*-B. Our French Baccalauréat students achieve an average score of 15/20, with a high percentage earning "mention très bien" (highest honors). These results reflect our commitment to academic excellence and have enabled our graduates to gain admission to top universities worldwide.

We offer a wide range of extracurricular activities to enrich student life and develop well-rounded individuals. These include sports (soccer, basketball, volleyball, tennis, swimming, athletics), arts (choir, orchestra, drama, visual arts), academic clubs (debate, Model UN, robotics, coding), and service initiatives (community service, environmental club). Activities take place after school and on weekends, with opportunities for local and international competitions and exchanges.

Yes, all students from Forms 1-5 are required to wear the school uniform, which consists of navy blue trousers or skirt, white shirt/blouse, school tie, and navy blue blazer with the school crest. Sixth Form students follow a business casual dress code. The uniform helps create a sense of community and equality among students. Detailed uniform guidelines are provided upon admission.

Our school cafeteria provides nutritious, balanced meals prepared fresh daily. Lunch is included in the tuition fees, with options for different dietary requirements (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, etc.). Breakfast and snacks are available for purchase. Our menu rotates on a three-week cycle and includes international cuisine that reflects our diverse community. Students may also bring packed lunches if they prefer.

We follow a positive discipline approach that emphasizes respect, responsibility, and character development. Our code of conduct is clearly communicated to all students and parents. Minor infractions are handled through a system of warnings and reflective conversations, while more serious issues involve parent conferences and appropriate consequences. We believe in restorative practices that help students learn from their mistakes and develop self-discipline.

Yes, international experiences are an important part of our program. We organize annual language immersion trips to French-speaking countries for students in Forms 3-6. We also have exchange programs with partner schools in France, Canada, and other countries, allowing students to experience different educational systems and cultures. Additionally, we participate in international competitions, conferences, and service projects that provide valuable global exposure.

Our campus features modern facilities designed to support academic excellence and holistic development. These include well-equipped classrooms with interactive technology, specialized science laboratories, computer labs, a library with resources in multiple languages, art studios, music rooms, a 400-seat auditorium, indoor sports hall, swimming pool, outdoor sports fields, and recreational areas. All facilities are regularly maintained and updated to provide an optimal learning environment.

Yes, our school library is a central hub for learning and research. It houses over 20,000 print books in English, French, and other languages, as well as digital resources including e-books, academic databases, and multimedia materials. The library is staffed by qualified librarians who provide research guidance and organize literacy events. Students have access to the library during school hours and for extended hours during examination periods.

Our sports facilities include a multi-purpose indoor sports hall for basketball, volleyball, badminton, and other indoor sports; a 25-meter swimming pool; outdoor fields for soccer, rugby, and athletics; tennis courts; and a fitness center for senior students. These facilities are used for physical education classes, school team practices, and recreational activities. They are also available for community use outside school hours.

We have dedicated spaces for creative expression, including art studios equipped for various media (painting, sculpture, ceramics, digital art), music rooms with instruments and recording equipment, a dance studio with mirrors and proper flooring, and a drama studio for theatrical productions. Our auditorium hosts performances throughout the year, showcasing student talent in music, drama, and dance.

Yes, we have a fully equipped health center staffed by qualified nurses during school hours. The health center provides first aid, manages student medications, conducts regular health screenings, and handles minor illnesses and injuries. For emergencies, we have protocols in place for immediate medical attention, including partnerships with nearby medical facilities. All staff are trained in basic first aid and emergency procedures.
